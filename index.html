Alejandro Scaffa

Deciphering the role of splicing in disease with high throughput techniques and CRISPR

In a nutshell...

I am a second year PhD student at the Department Molecular Pharmacology and Physiology, at Brown University. My advisor is William Fairbrother, Ph.D, and I do research in RNA splicing and CRISPR.

I am originally from Argentina but I lived in Brazil since age 8. I have a biochemistry degree from Grinnell College in Iowa.

Currently, I am doing research on how splicing plays a role in genetic diseases. I am working on developing better high throughput splicing assays to study thousands of genetic variants in one go. We are using CRISPR to generate model of diseases in human cell lines to further study splicing and prove the accuracy of the high throughput assays.
