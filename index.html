Alejandro Scaffa

Deciphering the Role of Hyperoxia Induced Senescence in Bronchopulmonary Dysplasia

In a nutshell...

I am a fourth year PhD student at the Department Molecular Pharmacology and Physiology, at Brown University. My advisor is Phyllis Dennery, M.D., and I do research in Bronchopulmonary Displasia (BPD) and Senescence. I have a strong background in genetics, CRISPR, and biochemistry.

I am originally from Argentina but I lived in Brazil since age 8. I have a biochemistry degree from Grinnell College in Iowa.

Currently, I am doing research on how hyperoxia induced senescence plays a role in bronchopulmonary displasia. In more detail, I am looking at how hyperoxia induced senescence changes the behavior of oncogene p53, a tumor supressor that helps induce senescence. I hope to understand the different roles of p53 on the metabolome and to further comprehend the role of these senescent cells in BPD.
