Alejandro Scaffa

Deciphering the Role of Hyperoxia Induced Senescence in Bronchopulmonary Dysplasia

In a nutshell...

I'm in my last year of PhD at the of Department Molecular Pharmacology and Physiology, at Brown University. My advisor is Phyllis Dennery, MD, and under her mentorship I research Bronchopulmonary Displasia (BPD), metabolism, and senescence.

I have a strong background in genetics, CRISPR, and biochemistry.

I'm curious, driven, a team player, and I'm passionate about innovative science.

I am originally from Argentina but I lived in Brazil since age 8. I have a biochemistry degree from Grinnell College in Iowa. and a Master's degree in molecular pharmacology and physiology from Brown University.

Currently, I am doing research on how hyperoxia induced senescence plays a role in bronchopulmonary displasia.

I'm open to job offers, eligible to work in USA, Argentina, Brazil, and Europe.
